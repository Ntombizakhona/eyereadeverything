# Focus Through Distraction

## Silence the Ping Monster
------------------------

The world is a dark dungeon of distractions. Your phone dings every five minutes, that new Netflix series keeps asking, “Are you still watching?” (yes, Netflix, I am, unfortunately), and don’t even get me started on the rabbit hole of memes on social media. It feels like our attention spans are being sliced thinner than deli meat. Yet, amid this chaos, the ability to focus and stay disciplined is what separates productive people from… well, the rest of us scrolling TikTok at midnight.

As Jim Rohn wisely said:

> You can either suffer the pain of discipline or the pain of regret.

And let’s be honest, discipline hurts less than realizing you’ve wasted a whole year _“planning to start tomorrow.”_

Recognize Your Distractions
---------------------------

The first step to building self-discipline is admitting you have a problem. Are you endlessly scrolling Instagram while your to-do list grows cobwebs? Or maybe you’re bingeing true crime podcasts while “multitasking”, also commonly referred to as doing nothing effectively, because you’re clearly not focused.

For some, it’s doom-scrolling through TikTok until their thumbs ache. For others, it’s “just one more” episode of a show they’re only half paying attention to. For me, it was my Netflix addiction. One minute, I’m promising myself I’ll only watch one episode, and the next, I’m emotionally invested in the third season of a show I didn’t even like.

Sound familiar? We’ve all got our distractions — those sneaky little habits that steal hours from our days and leave us wondering where the time went. Recognizing these distractions is the first step to reclaiming your focus.

Take a moment to think about your own routine. Are there specific times when you find yourself zoning out or procrastinating? Maybe it’s those endless “quick checks” of your phone that turn into a full-blown internet deep dive. Once you spot your time-wasters, you can start making intentional changes to get back in control.

So, what’s your version of “just one episode”? Whatever it is, it’s time to call it out and take charge.

Set Achievable Goals
--------------------

Nobody becomes a productivity guru overnight. Telling yourself, “I’m going to wake up at five, run ten kilometers, and finish three books before lunch,” is a surefire way to burn out before the day even begins. Instead, start small.

Want to read more? Forget about devouring an entire book, nibble ten pages a day. If you’re trying to exercise, don’t sign up for a whole marathon, you’re not [David Goggins](https://davidgoggins.com/about/)…yet, so go for a walk.

As Cal Newport puts it in _Deep Work_,

> Clarity about what matters provides clarity about what does not.

Focus on what really matters. You’ll be surprised how quickly small wins snowball into bigger ones.

Distraction-Free Zones
----------------------

Your environment can either help you crush it or completely derail you. If your “workspace” doubles as your “place where I snack and scroll,” you’ve got a problem. Even at school, the playground is not in the classroom for a reason.

### **Claim Your Territory**

Pick a spot, your desk, the kitchen table, wherever, and declare it a distraction-free zone. Or, if possible, go to the library, or wherever you know for sure you won’t be distracted by humans or machines.

### **Tidy Up**

Clutter isn’t just visual noise; it’s mental noise. A clean space helps your brain stay focused. Your desk should contain the essentials of work or study, nothing more, nothing less.

### **Silence the Ping Monster**

Turn off notifications, switch on ‘Do Not Disturb Mode’ to block the literal vortex of distractions. And no, you don’t _need_ to check your email 37 times a day. Twice a day is enough! Your phone is no longer your friend, it’s an insatiable Ping Monster demanding to have it’s belly rubbed everyday. Silence the Ping Monster!

Focus And Embrace Discipline
----------------------------

In _Deep Work_, Cal Newport explains that focusing on a single task without distractions is not just productive — it’s _rare._ And since rarity equals value, deep work is your secret weapon for standing out in a world where people can’t focus long enough to finish a YouTube ad.

Self-discipline isn’t glamorous. There are no fireworks when you turn off Netflix to read, and nobody’s clapping when you resist doom-scrolling. But trust me, future-you will be _so_ grateful for your current effort.

Once again, Jim Rohn was right:

> “You can either suffer the pain of discipline or the pain of regret.”

So why not go with the one that makes your life better?

Now, what’s one thing you’ll focus on today to start flexing your self-discipline muscle?

Celebrate Tiny Wins
-------------------

Building self-discipline is a marathon, not a sprint. And while it might feel like you’re crawling some days, every step forward matters. Finished a chapter? High-five yourself. Completed a focused work session? Treat yourself to a fancy coffee or a guilt-free Netflix binge (just don’t get stuck again).

Small wins keep you motivated. As Newport would say,

> “Success comes to those who value the deep, not the shallow.”

So celebrate the deep work — no matter how small it seems, but don’t over do it obviously.

BOOK RECOMMENDATION
-------------------

_Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World_ by Cal Newport is for anyone who wants to work smarter, not harder, in a world that constantly pulls us toward the shallow end of productivity, and teaches us about the importance of deeply focusing on the task at hand, while shutting out ‘distractions’.
